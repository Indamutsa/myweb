Jesus said: But seek first his kingdom and his righteousness, and all these things will be given to you as well.

Contentment in the Kingdom of God

Contentment for some is defined in terms of possessions: mansions, cars, fame, position, status quo, beautiful girlfriends or boyfriends, owning successful businesses and so on. Some also think they would have been content if they would have been born in certain parts of the world, family with this or that race, or if they were born of supernatural aptitude like Einstein or Solomon or Beethoven! All these things last for a small period and then it's gone and none remembers it. They all vanish, becoming dust. The ever wiser person ever lived on this planet is supposed to be a king called Solomon, and he said all that we toil up under the sun is simply chasing after the wind, all vanity!.
If these things were deserving what we have attributed unto it so far, famous artists, politicians, scientists, businesspersons, athletes would not commit suicide. Why? Because all that a human can ever desire they get hold of in this life. They live a utopia life as some thinks but still they end desperately, still not satisfied. Jesus said to Peter when he asked him what they will get back as compensation for all they left back, and He told him he will get a hundredfold of what he left and also get a splendid everlasting life. But as we scrutinize his life, we never saw him with a hundred boats, or with a hundred fishing industries, or with his business expanding in hundred countries. All twelve apostles lived in dire circumstances and died miserably, reason why they are called martyrs, that was nothing of prestige. No one lived with his family, they were just wandering off hills and valleys preaching the kingdom of God to the people who looked like sheep without a shepherd and when we inquire if they were content with that life, they thought nothing on earth could be compared of the treasure they had. One of them said things on earth are like Shit compared to the glory bestowed on them
Then what was Jesus really talking about? According to Experince and research: abundance of possessions only brings sorrow, fear and worry. And the bible says that the love of money (things) is the root of every evil. So then where does contentment come from ? My Pastor preached in the morning that it comes from focusing on Jesus. Psychologists says it comes from fixing your thoughts on good things and being optimistic, I say without Jesus we are fooling ourselves. That void stays.
Jesus said seek the kingdom of God and his righteousness, and then everything you want personally will be added unto. That was serious. Righteousness means keeping yourselves in alignment with the kingdom, accepting the grace of God. Believing what His Son did for us, and accepting his forgiveness of our sins. We no longer need to do anything to be accepted or forgiven. Don't do anything the kingdom consider wrong. Seek the kingdom of God means you invest your time and money to study it, to want to know, having thirst of knowing the mysteries of the kingdom and then you enter it. To stay within is simply your fixing your thoughts on whatever things are true, honest, just, pure, lovely, of good report, if there be any virtue and Praise, think on these things. Things you learnt, received and heard and seen in Jesus, do it and the God of peace shall be with you.
Philippians 4:8-9. That's how we stay in the kingdom seeking it which brings contentment. That's what Job had when he praised God after things and his children were gone, that's what three young men in Babylon had when they said we don't care of the fierce fire, that's what Paul and Silas had when they chose to sing while chained and that's what early Christians had when they accepted to be beaten, confiscated of their goods and dying left to hungry lions in arenas of Roma or burnt in public squares because of their Faith. Do you want to know what they had? Joshua 1:8-9, Matthew 7:7-8
